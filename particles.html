📊 Core Sentence Structure

Japanese Sentence Structure
Time (に)¹ Topic (は) Particles & Adverb Verb
Time (に) Object (を) Co-participant (と)
Subject (が) Means (で) Location (で)
Location of Existence (に) Origin (から) Destination (に)
Direction (へ) End-point (まで) Adverb
¹ Time (に) can go in front of Topic (は) but it is not always the case. The sentence could just start with Topic (は). Everything other than the verb is optional, including the topic. Order in which the particles appear in the sentence is not important, in that Japanese is very flexible with multiple ways to communicate the same information.

📋 Complete Particle Reference

All particles with their functions, positions, and examples from your N5 sentences. 🟢 N5 Example 🟡 Extra Example

は
Topic Marker
Position: After the topic
Defines the topic of the sentence. Emphasizes information AFTER は.
が
Subject Marker
Position: After the subject
Defines the doer of the action. Emphasizes information BEFORE が.
を
Object Marker
Position: After the direct object
Marks the direct object of an action verb.
に
Time / Direction / Existence
Position: After time, destination, or location
Indicates specific time, direction of movement, or location of existence.
で
Location / Means
Position: After place of action or method/instrument
Indicates where an action takes place or the means by which something is done.
へ
Direction Marker
Position: After direction of movement
Indicates the general direction of movement (pronounced "e").
と
And / With
Position: Between nouns (and) or after person (with)
Connects nouns or indicates accompaniment.
から
Origin / From
Position: After starting point (time or place)
Indicates origin, starting point in time or space.
まで
Until / To
Position: After end point (time or place)
Indicates an end point in time or space.
の
Possession / Linking
Position: Between two nouns (A の B)
Connects nouns to show possession, description, or relationship.
も
Also / Even
Position: Replaces は, が, を; adds to other particles
Indicates "also", "too", or "even".
か
Question / Or
Position: At end of sentence (question) or between nouns (or)
Marks a question or indicates choice ("or").
よ
Emphasis
Position: End of sentence
Adds emphasis, certainty, or new information.
ね
Confirmation
Position: End of sentence
Seeks agreement or confirmation ("isn't it?", "right?").
や
Partial Listing
Position: Between nouns
Lists examples ("things like...", "such as...").
